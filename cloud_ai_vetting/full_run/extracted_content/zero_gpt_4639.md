Human-Written Text
Text that genuinely sounds like it was written by a human. Human writing varies depending on purpose and context—sometimes casual and spontaneous, sometimes carefully crafted, but always with personal touches. Key characteristics include:
- Natural and flowing text with occasional minor imperfections, typical of human writing
- Features personal stories, unique expressions, and individual perspectives, making the writing distinct
- Clearly conveys genuine emotions and honesty, creating a relatable and authentic feel
- Writing style and pace vary naturally and dynamically, reflecting a human thought process
- Rich in specific details, personal examples, and context-relevant information that adds depth and originality
Mixed Content
Text that appears to contain both human-created and AI-generated portions, or heavily edited AI content. You might observe:
- Language style is inconsistent, mixing formal and informal phrasing within the text
- Sentence structure varies from simple to complex, sometimes abruptly
- Combines general information with hints of personal insights, but may lack depth in both
- Text generally flows naturally, but might contain minor grammatical errors or awkward phrasing
- Tone or feeling can shift unexpectedly, suggesting a blend of different writing styles
AI-Generated Text
Text that strongly appears to be AI-written. Humans rarely maintain this writing style consistently in normal circumstances. Common indicators include:
- Sentences are highly structured and grammatically perfect, sometimes to an unnatural degree
- Formal and sophisticated vocabulary is consistently used, potentially sounding overly academic
- Relies on common phrases and clichés, lacking unique or original expressions
- Writing style is uniform and predictable throughout the text, potentially feeling rigid
- Objective and factual tone, often missing personal opinions, emotions, or unique perspectives
It's important to note that certain types of professional writing (such as legal documents or academic papers) may be flagged as AI-like even when human-written, due to their formal nature.
