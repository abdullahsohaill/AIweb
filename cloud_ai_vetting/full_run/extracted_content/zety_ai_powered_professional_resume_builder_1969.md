The best resume format to use in 2026 is a reverse-chronological resume . It displays your information from the most recent to the oldest of your qualifications, highlighting the most relevant achievements on your resume . It’s also the most popular format preferred by recruiters.
However, depending on your career path, you may need to choose a skills-based resume (or functional resume ), which focuses more on your skills rather than work experience, or a combination resume , that mixes chronological and functional formats.
A well-formatted resume ensures recruiters can quickly scan your qualifications and helps you pass Applicant Tracking Systems (ATS) . Here are the top resume tips to format your document correctly:
- Use a Modern Template : Avoid tables, graphics, and multiple columns to ensure your resume passes ATS scans.
- Choose a Professional Font : Stick to Arial, Calibri, Helvetica, or Times New Roman in 10–12 pt for text and 14–16 pt for section headers.
- Keep Formatting Consistent: Use the same font, spacing, bullet points, and date formats throughout your resume.
- Use Bullet Points for Readability: Replace long paragraphs with concise bullet points starting with strong action verbs .
- Save Your Resume as a PDF : PDFs preserve formatting, but if a job posting requests a Word file , follow the instructions.
- Use Standard Section Headers: Stick to "Work Experience," "Education," and "Skills" to make your resume easy to scan.
- Set Proper Margins and Alignment: For a clean resume layout , keep margins between 0.5” and 1” and use left-aligned or justified text.
- Limit Resume Length : Use one page for entry-level candidates and two pages for professionals with extensive relevant experience.
- Highlight Key Information: Bold job titles, italicize company names, and use consistent formatting to improve readability.
- Proofread Before Submitting: Run a spell check, review formatting, and ensure there are no typos or inconsistencies. A polished resume makes a strong first impression and increases your chances of landing an interview.
