The types of knowledge management include tacit, explicit, and embedded knowledge. Tacit knowledge is personal and difficult to formalize; explicit knowledge is documented and easily shared; embedded knowledge is integrated into organizational processes and structures.
A Knowledge Management Framework is a structured plan that outlines how an organization captures, organizes, shares, and uses knowledge. It includes roles, processes, technologies, and governance to support effective knowledge management.
