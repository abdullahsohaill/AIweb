## 3 Things You Need to Rank on Page 1 and Drive Traffic

### 1. Beat the Best

Google doesn’t rank decent content. It ranks the best content available.
Which means... you have to outdo every single result already on Page 1.
How?
- Google your target keyword
- Study what the top-ranking pages are doing
- Create content that beats them on every level
If they cover 5 subtopics, you cover 10.
If they write 2,000 words, you write 3,000.
You also need to monitor the SERP, which changes a lot.
Some pages climb the rankings, others drop.
For pages that went up: what did they do that Google liked?
You need to find out what they did that worked and replicate it.

### 2. Get Google to Trust You

Google doesn’t hand out rankings to random one-off articles.
It rewards websites that demonstrate topical authority and E-E-A-T.
That means building trust through consistent, in-depth coverage of your niche.
You need to:
- Cover your niche comprehensively
- Build content clusters
- Interlink them strategically
- Become the go-to source for your topic
When Google sees your site as the most helpful, authoritative, and relevant source, it ranks you higher.

### 3. Stand Out With Uniqueness

The internet is now full of recycled content and generic AI slop.
If you want to stand out – and actually rank – you need to offer something different.
Here’s how you do it:
- Deliver information gain – don’t just reword what’s already out there; add new angles, insights, or data
- Demonstrate original thinking or expertise – share unique perspectives, real-world experience, or niche knowledge that only you (or your brand) can offer
- Write like a human – with a voice that makes people want to keep reading, not a robot with no feelings
If your content feels mass-produced or adds nothing new, both readers and Google will ignore you.
