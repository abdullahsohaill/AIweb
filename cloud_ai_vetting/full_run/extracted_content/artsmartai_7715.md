### Not Just a Pretty UI Over Some API Calls

Dear readers,
Long before I became a SaaS founder, I used to run a t-shirt graphic design shop. Customers would ask me for designs and we’d print them on t-shirts. Pretty straight forward, except for the fact that I dealt with a common problem faced by designers of any kind:
Customers always have a problem with the designs you send them.
I’d design art and send it to the customer. They’d not like some part of the design and ask for changes. But that’s an additional $50/hour for design work that the customer wasn’t willing to pay for. So we’d end up losing a lot of business over customers thinking they know design better than professional designers.
Some years later, I got into SEO. And when doing SEO, search engines rank web pages higher the longer a reader spends on the page. One way to increase “time on page” is to make the content interactive by including custom images like graphs, infographics, designs etc.
But good designers are expensive and they work SLOW. We could write a fantastic article in hours but graphic design took days.
Plus, much of the SEO work I did was generating traffic for SaaS products. One day I realized, hey, if I’m so good at getting traffic for SaaS products, why don’t I build my own? That’s how I became a SaaS founder.
ArtSmart AI isn’t my first SaaS but it’s the one I’m most excited about. And lucky for us, anything to do with AI is mui caliente right now. So the growth ArtSmart AI has seen since launch is unreal.
But don’t get the wrong idea. We aren’t some fly-by-night entrepreneurs looking to cash-in on the AI wave. My team and I are all-in on ArtSmart AI for the long haul. Our users are an endless well of ideas, so we’re shipping new features almost at breakneck speed. And since we’re part of the open source community, ArtSmart AI improves with every update to the models we built and trained out software on.
The generative AI space is brand new and new apps are launching every week. We’re witnessing history in the making and we’re honored to play our part in it. We invite you to join us on this journey.
