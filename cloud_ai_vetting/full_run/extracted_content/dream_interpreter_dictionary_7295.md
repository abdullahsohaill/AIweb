## Why do we have dreams?

Have you ever wondered why we have dreams when we sleep ? You're not alone; even scientists and psychologists have been trying to figure it out for a long time! One idea is that our brain uses dreams kind of like a computer running a "disk cleanup". At night, our brain sorts through all the things we learned and felt during the day and decides what to remember and what to forget. This is a bit like cleaning up old files on a computer to make it run better.
Another cool thought is that dreams are like practice sessions. Think of them as a safe space where you can "rehearse" or go over different situations. Ever had a dream where you faced a fear, like speaking in front of the class? That might be your brain's way of helping you get ready for real-life challenges.
Lastly, dreams might have once helped our ancestors be safer. Imagine dreaming about running away from a big wild animal; this dream might have helped our ancient relatives practice staying safe! So, even if dreams can sometimes be weird or confusing, they're a sign that our brains are working hard, even when we're sleeping.

## Why do my dreams feel real?

It's fascinating how dreams can feel so real , almost like you're living another life while you sleep, right? There's a good reason for that. When you're dreaming, especially during a part of sleep called "REM" (which stands for Rapid Eye Movement), your brain is super active. It's kind of like when you're awake and doing things, but your body stays still because of a neat trick your brain does to keep you safe in bed.
During these dreams, the parts of your brain that deal with feelings and experiences light up, just like they would if you were really living the dream. So if you're flying, your brain is making you feel that amazing sensation of soaring through the air. If you're eating a giant ice cream cone, your brain makes it taste super yummy.
But here's the interesting part: while all this is happening, the part of your brain that uses logic takes a little break. That's why you might not question why you're suddenly on the moon or talking to a talking cat. Without your "logic center" working at full speed, everything in your dream, no matter how wild, feels 100% real!

## How long do dreams last?

Most of our dreams happen during a special part of our sleep called the REM stage. If you ever watched someone sleep and noticed their eyes moving quickly under their eyelids, they're probably in the REM stage.
Now, the first time we enter REM in the night, the dream might last just a few minutes, kind of like a short YouTube video. But as the night goes on and we get more and more sleep, the REM stages get longer. So, by the morning, a dream can be as long as a full episode of your favorite TV show, about 20-30 minutes.
It's interesting though, because sometimes our dreams might feel like they lasted a whole day or even longer. That's just another one of the cool tricks our brain plays on us with time perception. Even if a dream seems super long, it's often much shorter when we look at the clock. Learn more about how long dreams last .
