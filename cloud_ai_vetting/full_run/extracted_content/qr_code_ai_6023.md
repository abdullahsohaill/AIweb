## Frequently Asked Questions About Artistic QR Codes

### Understanding QR Codes

A QR code is a two-dimensional barcode that, when scanned by a smartphone or QR code reader, instantly links users to online content, websites, contact info, and more. Beyond simple links, QR codes can embed advanced functionalities like payment gateways, location sharing, event RSVPs, and multimedia files making them ideal for quick and seamless information sharing across marketing, events, and product interactions.
The father of the QR code is Masahiro Hara , an engineer at Denso Wave, invented the QR code in 1994 in Japan to address limitations of barcodes, particularly in the automotive industry, by creating a two-dimensional code with high storage capacity and quick readability. Denso Wave holds the patent but chose not to exercise it, making the QR code freely accessible.
A QR code is a two-dimensional matrix storing data both horizontally and vertically, allowing it to hold more information and be scanned from any angle. In contrast, a barcode is one dimensional, storing data only horizontally, which limits its storage and requires specific alignment for scanning.
QR codes are essential today for instant, contactless access to digital content, connecting users to online information in seconds. They’re commonly seen in restaurant menus , event check-ins , and COVID-19 vaccination passes . In marketing, QR codes drive engagement creatively, as with Taylor Swift’s giant QR code in Chicago to tease her album launch. Their ease of use and versatility across industries make them a go-to tool in our digital-first world.
