### Distance from the camera

Take the photo from a distance of about 20 inches from your face. Ideally, the photo should be taken by another person.

Place your face in front of the lens and keep a neutral expression on your face. Hold the camera at face height.

Passport photos must meet specific requirements .
For digital photos, they should be clear, in focus, unaltered by computer software, and taken against a plain, light-coloured background. The photo must be at least 600 pixels wide and 750 pixels tall, and between 50KB and 10MB in size.
For printed photos, they should measure 45mm high by 35mm wide, be printed on high-quality paper, and the image of you (from the crown of your head to your chin) must be between 29mm and 34mm high.
The following are not accepted in a passport photo:
- Photos that do not meet the size and quality requirements
- Photos with other objects or people
- Photos taken against a non-plain or dark background
- Photos with 'red eye'
- Photos where the subject is not facing forwards or looking straight at the camera
- Photos with hair covering the eyes or face
- Photos with head coverings, unless for religious or medical reasons, that obscure the face
- Photos with shadows on the face or background
- Wearing sunglasses or tinted glasses
- Any alterations made using computer software
- Photos with a non-neutral expression, such as smiling or frowning
