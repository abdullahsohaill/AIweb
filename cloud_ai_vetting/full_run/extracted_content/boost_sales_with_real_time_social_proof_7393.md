## What is Social Proof

Social proof is the idea that people look at what others are doing to decide how they should act in a situation. In simple terms, if others are doing something, we feel we should do it too. This happens most when we’re unsure of what to do and believe others might know better. Also called “herd mentality,” social proof makes us trust choices that many others have made. It’s why we ask for advice, think crowded restaurants must be good, and why businesses show customer reviews and testimonials. In digital marketing, it means people are more likely to read, share, or engage with your content if they see others doing it first.

## How can eCommerce use Social Proof?

A common example of social proof in online shopping is showing how popular a product is by displaying the number of views, items added to carts, or purchases made in a certain time period. For instance, a message like “83 people bought this product in the last 24 hours” makes customers feel a sense of urgency and fear of missing out. Knowing that others have already bought the product helps customers feel more confident in their decision to buy it too. When people are unsure, they feel reassured by seeing that others have made the same choice. By creating the feeling that everyone is buying the product, more customers are likely to follow. In today’s competitive market, even if a product is great, social proof can make a big difference in convincing people to buy.
