### Can You Take Your Own Passport Photo?

Generally, it’s perfectly legal to take your own passport photo — provided that it meets the official standards for size, background, lighting, and facial position. The final decision always belongs to the issuing authority, such as the U.S. Department of State , HM Passport Office , or other national agencies. However, some jurisdictions, such as Canada, require only professionally taken passport-size photographs, according to the Immigration, Refugees and Citizenship Canada (IRCC) guidelines.
For U.S. and U.K. applications, photos must be captured against a real backdrop without AI or digital editing. ### Can You Smile in a Passport Photo?

No. General passport photo requirements don't allow smiling. A neutral expression with no smiling or frowning is acceptable. Your eyes should be open, your mouth closed, and your gaze directed straight into the camera. A “neutral facial expression” is required by the International Civil Aviation Organization for the process of biometric identification.

### What to Wear for Passport Photo?

Wear everyday clothes in darker colors that don't blend with the white backgdrop. Avoid patterns, logos, or text. Keep your hair away from your face. Hats, accessories that obstruct the facial features, aren't permitted on identification document photographs. Head coverings worn daily for religious or medical reasons are allowed — but only if they don't obstruct the applicant's face.

### Can I wear glasses in a passport photo?

It depends on the country that issues the document.
Glasses aren’t permitted in official IDs in the U.S. and the European Union. However, they are acceptable in India, the UK, and Canada, as long as the eyes are clearly visible and the lenses show no glare or light reflection. Always check the identification document photograph guidelines of the issuing country before applying.
