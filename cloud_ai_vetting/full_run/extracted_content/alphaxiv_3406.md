## Ask or search anything...

Researchers introduce TTT-Discover, a test-time training framework that enables Large Language Models to learn and adapt during problem-solving, leading to new state-of-the-art solutions in diverse scientific and engineering challenges. The method achieved improved mathematical bounds, faster GPU kernels, and top scores in programming competitions using an open-source model at low computational cost.
[LINK: 278](https://github.com/sayantann11/all-classification-templetes-for-ML)
Researchers from NVIDIA and Stanford University fine-tune a large, pretrained latent video diffusion model, Cosmos-Predict2, into a unified robot policy for visuomotor control and planning. This method achieves state-of-the-art average success rates of 98.5% on LIBERO, 67.1% on RoboCasa, and 93.6% on ALOHA, demonstrating enhanced data efficiency and robustness for complex manipulation tasks.
Researchers from Renmin University of China and Microsoft Research introduced LLM-in-Sandbox, a framework that integrates Large Language Models with a lightweight virtual computing environment to enhance agentic capabilities across diverse non-code domains. This approach led to strong LLMs achieving substantial performance gains and enabled new multimodal content creation, while also demonstrating an 8x reduction in token consumption for long-context tasks.
The Qwen Team introduced Qwen3-TTS, a series of text-to-speech models that use a novel dual-track language model architecture and specialized tokenizers to achieve high-quality, multilingual, and controllable speech synthesis. The models demonstrate strong performance in voice cloning, natural language instruction-based voice design, and ultra-low first-packet latency of 97 milliseconds.
[LINK: 842](https://github.com/QwenLM/Qwen3-TTS)
Meituan, in collaboration with Fudan, Tongji, and HKUST, developed EvoCUA, an evolutionary framework for computer-use agents that learns from scalable synthetic experience instead of static datasets. This approach achieved a 56.7% success rate on the OSWorld benchmark, establishing a new open-source state-of-the-art and nearing the performance of leading closed-source models.
[LINK: 117](https://github.com/meituan/EvoCUA)
New York University researchers scaled Representation Autoencoders (RAEs) for large-scale text-to-image generation, demonstrating superior performance over Variational Autoencoders (VAEs) in convergence speed, generation quality, and resistance to overfitting. This framework also unifies visual understanding and generation within a shared semantic space, enabling advanced multimodal reasoning.
A comprehensive survey from Shanghai AI Lab and collaborating institutions systematically categorizes and analyzes efficiency strategies for large language model (LLM)-based agents across their memory, tool learning, and planning components. The work defines efficient agents as those that maximize task success while minimizing resource consumption like token usage and latency, identifying critical bottlenecks and outlining future research directions for practical deployment.
Purdue University, ByteDance, and NUS researchers developed SAMTok, a framework that converts any image segmentation mask into two discrete language tokens, allowing multimodal large language models (MLLMs) to achieve pixel-wise understanding and generation through standard next-token prediction. This non-intrusive approach enhances MLLMs like QwenVL with state-of-the-art capabilities in various vision-language tasks, including interleaved text-mask generation and multi-round interactive segmentation, while simplifying their training process.
Google DeepMind researchers developed "360Anything," a geometry-free framework that converts standard perspective images and videos into 360
° panoramas using Diffusion Transformers, demonstrating state-of-the-art quality and implicit geometric understanding while eliminating visual seams.
Stable-DiffCoder leverages diffusion-based continual pretraining to enhance the capability of code Large Language Models, demonstrating superior performance over its autoregressive counterpart (Seed-Coder) across various code generation, reasoning, and editing benchmarks, particularly in low-resource languages and complex problem-solving tasks.
Researchers at Stanford University developed Generalized Optimal transport Attention with Trainable priors (GOAT), which reframes the attention mechanism as an Entropic Optimal Transport problem with an explicit, learnable prior distribution. This approach yields improved in-distribution perplexity on language models, 36% lower peak memory allocation in biological sequence modeling, and robust extrapolation capabilities across language, vision, and biology tasks.
An independent researcher introduces a framework to control long-horizon behavior in LLM agents by imposing explicit, physically-inspired dynamical structures on an external, low-dimensional affective state. This approach enables agents to exhibit enhanced temporal coherence, controlled recovery, and predictable emotional transitions over multi-turn interactions, overcoming limitations of purely reactive or memory-based systems.
Researchers developed "LoL," a method to enable real-time, infinite-length video generation by mitigating "sink-collapse," a stability issue where autoregressive models revert to initial content. This training-free Multi-Head RoPE Jitter allowed for generating continuous video streams up to 12 hours with sustained quality.
BayesianVLA proposes a framework to combat the "vision shortcut" in Vision-Language-Action (VLA) models by enforcing robust language grounding through Bayesian decomposition and maximizing conditional Pointwise Mutual Information. This method demonstrates an 11.3% absolute improvement in out-of-distribution generalization on the SimplerEnv benchmark and preserves the underlying VLM's general reasoning abilities.
LongCat-Flash-Thinking-2601 is an open-source 560-billion-parameter Mixture-of-Experts model developed by the Meituan LongCat Team, designed to enhance agentic reasoning and robustness in large language models. The model achieves state-of-the-art open-source performance on agentic search, tool use, and tool-integrated mathematical reasoning benchmarks, exhibiting up to 79.5% on real-world search tasks and 88.2% on tool-use benchmarks while demonstrating strong robustness to environmental noise.
A comprehensive survey details the rapidly evolving landscape of Large Language Model agents, reframing LLMs as autonomous entities capable of planning, acting, and learning through interaction. It introduces a structured taxonomy for agentic reasoning, encompassing foundational capabilities, self-evolving adaptation, and collective intelligence, while also reviewing diverse applications and identifying critical open challenges.
[LINK: 333](https://github.com/XiaoYee/Awesome_Efficient_LRM_Reasoning)
AnyView, developed by researchers at Toyota Research Institute and Amazon Web Services, enables the synthesis of perceptually realistic 4D videos from arbitrary viewpoints within dynamic scenes. The framework demonstrates robust novel view generation under extreme camera displacements, achieving state-of-the-art performance on the new AnyViewBench benchmark and surpassing existing baselines without relying on explicit 3D reconstruction.
Researchers from Fudan University, Shanghai AI Laboratory, and The Chinese University of Hong Kong developed TL-GRPO, a reinforcement learning algorithm to optimize large language model agents for reasoning-guided iterative optimization. The method achieved state-of-the-art performance in analog circuit sizing, scoring 0.97 in-domain and 0.35 out-of-domain, demonstrating superior generalization compared to traditional methods and larger general-purpose LLMs.
ActionMesh introduces a two-stage, feed-forward framework for generating production-ready, topology-consistent animated 3D meshes from various inputs. It achieves this by extending 3D diffusion models with temporal modeling, reducing animation generation time to approximately 3 minutes per 16-frame video while improving geometric accuracy by 21% and temporal consistency by 46% over prior methods.
The Render-of-Thought (RoT) framework transforms textual Chain-of-Thought steps into images for visual latent reasoning, achieving 3-4x token compression and substantial inference acceleration. This approach provides explicit supervision for intermediate reasoning, addressing the opacity of prior implicit methods.
