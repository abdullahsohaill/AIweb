## Featured Collections

### Connected Biology

This collection includes curriculum activities, tutorials, extension activities, and teacher resources for learning about the genetics of deer mice and the evolution of garden peas.

### Wildfire Risks & Impacts

Students use Wildfire Explorer to investigate the variables influencing fire spread, the risks wildfires bring to people, and the impact of climate change.

### Climate: High-Adventure Science

This set of student and teacher resources includes activities, assessments, and teacher guides addressing the question “What is the future of Earth's climate?"

## Standards

Feedback mechanisms maintain a living system's internal conditions within certain limits and mediate behaviors, allowing it to remain alive and functional even as external conditions change within some range. Feedback mechanisms can encourage (through positive feedback) or discourage (negative feedback) what is going on inside the living system.
Modeling in 9–12 builds on K–8 and progresses to using, synthesizing, and developing models to predict and show relationships among variables between systems and their components in the natural and designed worlds. Use a model based on evidence to illustrate the relationships between systems or between components of a system.
The functions and properties of natural and designed objects and systems can be inferred from their overall structure, the way their components are shaped and used, and the molecular substructures of its various materials.
