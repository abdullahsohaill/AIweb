## Bagger Ultra 25L Backpack

The Bagger Ultra 25L is the ultimate small travel backpack to take you from plane, to town, to trail. It is sized to fit under airplane seats and can even count as your personal item on budget airlines. With the right gear list you can bag a peak or two on a daytrip excursion or even do a couple overnights with a Summer Quilt , an ultralight tent, and room to spare. Featuring a small and streamlined design, the Bagger can be packed as a secondary backpack for summit side trips or used as an ultralight minimalist pack for short adventures into the backcountry. The Bagger utilizes an included, easily removable foam sit pad that doubles as a frame and padding against your back.
With 3 different interchangeable strap designs, a removable belt, a comfy sit pad, and a bunch of cool modular add-ons , the versatile Bagger Ultra is your ticket to maximizing day hikes, weekend trips, or commutes.

- The pack should keep water out in typical backpacking conditions where the pack is not submerged or exposed to prolonged periods of heavy rain. We still suggest using dry bags or a pack liner to protect important items like your clothing and sleeping bags.

- The top of the pack features a small zippered pocket, creating the perfect place for storing your phone, keys, or wallet.
- Side pockets are sized to fit one or two 1 liter bottles each. They are positioned low on the pack and slanted forward for easy access without removing the pack.
- A stretchy external sit pad pocket with a zipper is located on the back of the pack and allows you to easily remove your sit pad on breaks. The sleeve is also a convenient spot to tuck a small laptop or tablet behind the pad when used as a travel bag.

### Torso Height:

This backpack has a single belt attachment position. The actual distance on the pack from the bottom of the belt to the top of the shoulder straps is about about 18" (46 cm), which will fit most adults. On a frameless backpack, it is nice to have the shoulder straps roughly level with the tops of your shoulders (rather than wrapping up around them) so that more weight is forced on to the webbing belt. The pack body does not extend much above your shoulders and load lifter straps are not needed.

Standard Straps

The straps have a daisy chain for clipping on items like our popular shoulder pouches , shoulder pouches w/ zipper , water bottle sleeves , an adjustable position sternum strap, and removable load lifter straps are included. Vest Straps

### Belt Size:

The webbing belt will fit up to around 50" circumference and can be trimmed down for smaller waists or swapped out for larger waists. To move the belt to a different position, slide off the belt buckle and back the webbing out of the backpack attachment points.
3.3oz/sqyd Ultra 100X
.3.5 oz/sqyd Mesh
This is the mesh that we use on our backpack's center pockets. It has a relatively light weight for mesh, while being difficult to tear. It can withstand getting caught on branches and other typical abrasion that backpacks are subjected to. The hole size is a about 4 mm (a little over 1/8").
5.3 oz/sqyd 3D Spacer Mesh
This mesh has a three-dimensional structure which allows airflow and adds cushioning. We use it on the underside of our backpack 's shoulder straps and belts to absorb sweat, add cushion, and add grip. Our 3D mesh weighs less than most other types.

### Bagger Is A Winner

Before I got the bagger I was losing heart about finding a smaller pack to fit a smaller person in advance of a trip to the mountains of Peru. But this is the bag. (Stakes high because my 2015 purchased Arc Haul 40L is the most comfortable pack I’ve used in 50 years of backpacking and hiking.) I needed something smaller for shoving under a plane seat and to cover miles comfortably around town or on trail with big water bottles and room for lots of layers. With the addition of Nero waist belt (more on that below) this is a wonderful snug-to-the-back carry. Belt cushy. The top pocket opens forward so I am able to reach up and unzip to access phone, maps, sunscreen without removing the pack. My z-fold hiking poles fit easily into the front / outer zip pocket (which I prefer over a stretchy mesh open-top pocket). The mouth opening of the biggest compartment is ample for loading and unloading. I didn't start out this search thinking I'd find the perfect fit. Relieved and delighted by results.

The Bagger seemed *maybe* possible but I’m dealing with 1) being in my 70s with less cushion in joints plus upper body injuries so I need a good hipbelt and 2) being 5’ and 103 lbs with a 16” back length (Bagger is 18”); many packs are simply too big. For months I've been searching in stores & ordering online and returning packs. An exchange with founder/owner Joe Velasko led me to order the Bagger and Nero medium hip-belt and try it out. Took some time and patience to attach the hip-belt but the result is better than expected. (Z-Packs is in process of improving the Bagger's attach points and day pack padded belt). The Bagger hugs my back just as one wants and the padded belt is cushy. I’m super happy. Have hiked all over town, jamming groceries in it, threw it under a plane seat as my only luggage for three nights away, and have set out on long day hikes in the Sierra Nevada. I did not miss my Arc Haul on any of these outings which is saying a lot. I'm all set for the slopes of Huascaran and whatever life throws my way. I like the simplicity of Z-packs; the not over-built ethos suits me. Again, a winner bag.

### Umbrella Holster

This item has saved many a hike through scorching temperatures and inclement weather. Great product. Will never hit the trail without it.
– Christian D., ★★★★★
The Umbrella Holster allows you to keep your hands free while protecting yourself from the rain and sun with an ultralight umbrella. It easily attaches to the daisy chain on most backpack's straps. In calm weather you can keep both hands on your trekking poles, while in strong wind or driving rain you can rest one hand on the umbrella to control the angle.
The Umbrella Holster consists of a clip and an adjustable shock cord loop that attaches to your shoulder strap.
Attach the buckle above the sternum strap on your strap's daisy chain and then attach the shock cord loop at the bottom of the daisy chain. Insert your umbrella into the clip and then cinch the shock cord tight around the umbrella’s handle. Umbrella Holsters are compatible with Shoulder Pouches- the attachments can be positioned above and below the pouch, and the handle will ride to the inner side of the pouch. Consider purchasing two holsters for both straps so that you can easily move the umbrella to your left or right side depending on where the sun or rain is coming from.
