The Ohio Bureau of Motor Vehicles is warning residents of a texting scam that is posing as an official Ohio BMV message.
The department says residents have reported receiving text messages from scammers claiming to be from the BMV and saying the recipient has an outstanding traffic ticket.
The message then instructs the recipient to pay immediately to avoid a license suspension.
BMV officials say this phishing scam is being reported by drivers nationwide and is designed to trick the recipient into giving up personal or financial information.
“If you receive this text, do not fall for this scam,” said Ohio BMV Registrar Charlie Norman in a statement from the department. “Do not click any links and delete the text. Ohio BMV will never send you a text demanding payment or requesting your personal information.”
If you receive a this text, or one like it, you can report the scam attempt to the Federal Trade Commission by clicking here .
If you believe you have been a victim of a scam, contact your local law enforcement agency.
