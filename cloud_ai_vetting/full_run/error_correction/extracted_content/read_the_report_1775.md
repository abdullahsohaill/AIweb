T h e e c o n o m i c p o t e n t i a l o f v e r t i c a l A I
A cross-industry analysis of modeled business value based on operational outcomes from 80+ real-world deployments
AI is already delivering measurable impact in the enterprise. Across industries, it is streamlining operations, improving decision-making, and creating value in ways that were only theoretical a few years ago. This report begins with documented results and extrapolates their potential when applied more broadly across similar organizations and use cases.
We analyzed current deployments in retail, financial services, industrial manufacturing, and enterprise IT, focusing on specific operational applications, from predictive maintenance to fraud detection. As such, this analysis reflects the impact of the most common AI use cases in operation today, not every possible use case, real or projected, and not survey-based estimates, but what’s actually in production. Each projection is grounded in real-world data and scaled using conservative assumptions to model the potential economic contribution of AI in these contexts.
By anchoring the calculation in real data, this analysis provides a foundation for conversations around AI’s role in the enterprise. The figures in this report are not the ceiling of possibility for AI; they reflect the impact achievable today if industries leaned into AI at scale.

About this analysis
This study was conducted by SymphonyAI and draws on findings from 83 case studies across four industries. 

Outcomes were mapped to the business functions they support (e.g., merchandising, compliance, maintenance), quantified using operational benchmarks, and modeled for representative company sizes (e.g., $10B retailer). 

Scaled impact was then calculated using published counts of market sizing.
Modeling principles
Transparent, simple scaling logic 
Example: $1M saved per promotion × 50 promotions/year = $50M
Conservative institutional counts 
Example: 1,500 grocers in U.S. + EU
No double-counting between drivers 
labor savings ≠ infrastructure savings
