## 3.5 years later, 'Pop Tart Gun' suspension resolved

by Paul Gessler
ANNAPOLIS, Md. ( WBFF ) -- The disciplinary case of a then-7-year-old shaping his breakfast pastry into the shape of a gun in 2013 has been resolved.
Joshua Welch was a second grader at Park Elementary School when he was suspended for two days.
"It was already a rectangle and i just kept on biting it and biting it and tore off the top and it kinda looked like a gun but it wasn't," Joshua said at the time. "All I was trying to do was turn it into a mountain, but it didn't look like a mountain really, and it turned out to be a gun, kind of."
Welch has since switched schools and is now a middle school student, still in Anne Arundel County.
"Kids in fifth grade or younger should not be suspended at all unless there's serious personal injury or an actual weapon," attorney Robin Ficker said. "This should have been resolved at our very first meeting at the elementary school and then the school board wouldn't have wasted so much money fighting this."
A spokesperson for Anne Arundel County Schools confirmed the settlement, but could not comment any further.
