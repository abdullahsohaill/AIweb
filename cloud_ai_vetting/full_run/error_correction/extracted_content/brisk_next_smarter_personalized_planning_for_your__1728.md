Brisk Next is a central hub where you can quickly find, create, and organize everything you need to deliver a great lesson.

Simply browse smart suggestions tailored to your grade and standards, upload your own lesson plans, or even chat with Brisk's AI to refine the exact materials you need.

Already a Brisk user? You can skip this section.

- Create an account with your Google or Microsoft login credentials
- Make sure you install the Brisk extension for Google Chrome or the Brisk add-on for Microsoft Edge . Once it's installed, you'll start seeing the little Brisk "B" icon in the bottom right corner in your browser whenever Brisk is available.

Once you have a free Brisk account and have downloaded the Chrome extension, you can get to Brisk Next in two ways (image above):

## What resources can Brisk Next create?

Teaching has always been a balancing act of preparing engaging lessons and effectively assessing student learning. With Brisk Next, you now have a single, powerful tool to master all three. It’s a complete solution designed by and for educators, putting an end to endless searching and resource creation. Brisk Next streamlines the entire process, so you can focus on what matters most: your students.

## Use Next to create resources to PREPARE learners

Planning lessons has never been easier. When you tell Brisk what you're teaching next, it immediately suggests a range of materials to help you prepare. This could be a comprehensive presentation to introduce a new topic, guided notes that keep students on track during a lecture, or a standards-based worksheet that gets them ready for the day's lesson. You have everything you need to build a solid foundation for your students before they even walk in the door, all aligned to your specific standards and grade level.
Here are some ideas:

### Morning Meeting Routine Guide - Kindergarten

Create a step-by-step guide for implementing daily morning meetings that build community and establish predictable routines for kindergarten students.

### Reflection Planning Worksheet - 4th Grade

Develop guided notes for teachers to plan how students will reflect on their behavior and growth related to classroom expectations.

### Transition Procedures Guide - 7th Grade

Create a step-by-step guide for teaching students smooth transitions between activities, classes, and different learning spaces.

### Peer Support Network Guide - 11th Grade

Develop systems for students to support each other academically and socially while maintaining teacher oversight and guidance.

## Use Next to create resources to ENGAGE learners

Engaging students is key to a dynamic classroom, and Brisk Next is designed to help you do just that. It suggests interactive activities that bring lessons to life, like a podcast that explores a topic in a fresh way or a "Character Chat" that lets students have a conversation with a historical figure or a character from a book. These activities transform passive learning into an active, conversational experience that boosts critical thinking and keeps students excited about the material.
Here are some ideas:

### School Mascot Chat - Kindergarten

Students talk with their school mascot character about expectations for hallway walking, cafeteria behavior, and playground rules.

### Mystery Learning Hook - 4th Grade

Launch an intriguing mystery or puzzle that immediately captures student curiosity and sets up the day's learning objective.

### Peer Partnership Ideas - 7th Grade

Generate creative ways for students to connect with classmates and establish supportive learning relationships throughout the year.

### Social Dynamics Inquiry - 11th Grade

Investigate how different factors influence group dynamics and collaboration through guided scientific questioning and hypothesis formation.

## Use Next to create resources to ASSESS learners

When it's time to check for understanding, Brisk Next has you covered with a variety of assessment tools. You can quickly generate a quiz to see what students have learned, create a comprehensive rubric to provide clear expectations for an upcoming project, or use an exit ticket to get a quick snapshot of student comprehension. This allows you to gather meaningful data effortlessly, so you can see where your students are succeeding and where they might need a little extra support.
Here are some ideas:

### Daily Check-In Ticket - Kindergarten

Students complete a simple exit ticket about how they're feeling in their new classroom and what they learned today.

### Social Skills Inventory - 4th Grade

Students take an interactive assessment about their comfort with classroom collaboration, problem-solving, and communication skills.

### Study Skills Inventory - 7th Grade

Quick assessment of student organization habits, homework strategies, and time management skills developed over the summer.

### Skills Check Quiz - 11th Grade

Students take an interactive quiz to self-assess their readiness for grade 11 coursework and identify strengths and growth areas.

## Make a whole teaching unit with Brisk Bundles

When you're teaching a new topic, you often need more than just one resource. That's where Brisk Bundles come in! It’s all about putting together a full set of resources in a single click.
You can mix and match different Brisk-generated materials—like a presentation, a podcast, a guided notes page, and a quiz—all in one place. Just select the resources you want to use, click to add them to your bundle, and Brisk organizes everything for you.
Once your bundle is created, all of your materials are neatly grouped together. You can preview each one to make sure it's just right, and when you're ready, share the entire bundle with your students. It's the easiest way to make sure your lesson is comprehensive and ready to go.
